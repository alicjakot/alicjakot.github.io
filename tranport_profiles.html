Transport Profiles of England and Wales

Overview
In this project I was a part of a group working towards inspiring a dialogue about transport modes, availability and choice in England and Wales. The aim of our analysis and website was to be able to understand how transport usage within England and Wales differs and what factors are likely to affect these differences. A deeper understanding of the geographical differences in transport choice could inform national and local travel policies to move towards more sustainable modes of transport. I was a part of a web team, where I was responsible for the interactive map page and visualisations, demonstrating the results of our findings.

Our method
To understand the transportation characteristics across England and Wales, we used a clustering methodology to identify different groups of transport profiles. This analysis has been performed at the MSOA level given that this is the lowest level of geographic aggregation for which data was available. Subsequently, a classification analysis was performed to understand what demographic factors are related to these profiles. The results of our analysis and the visualisations show how transport usage profiles change across England and Wales. The map could be accessed here. 

Website, interactive map and visualisations
We presented our analysis as a website, which includes a story page that guides the reader through the analysis process and findings, and a map page where the user can further interact with the data and the results. The website’s aesthetic and structure were developed using HTML5 and CSS3.  JavaScript was used throughout the website to visualize data from the analysis and to create elements of interactivity. Our interactive map utilised Mapbox’s vector tiles API to display the clustering and flows, Mapbox studio to create a base map in our colour palette, and the geocoder API to provide a reliable search bar. We used D3.js to create dynamic and interactive data visualisations.

This project was originally written as part of a master's degree in the Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis, Bartlett Faculty of the Build Environment, UCL and was awarded a distinction.
